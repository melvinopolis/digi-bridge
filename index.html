Advocacy

Digi-Bridge uses a community organizing model to ignite conversation and action within neighborhoods, working with school districts to create model K-12 settings with technology-rich course offerings. We work to secure capital (both financial and human) to support our advocacy efforts.

The problem facing educators is access to the resources and supports necessary to empower this generation of learners. Digi-Bridge tackles this issue of access in an effort to move our nation towards an educational landscape where every student is afforded a quality, 21st century learning experience. There is no advocacy group in Charlotte specifically focused on issues of digital access (access to low-cost hardware/software, school based computer science education courses, and ongoing tutorials).

Consulting

Schools don't have an impartial, unaffiliated voice supporting them as they determine what technology resources should be introduced into the learning environment. Further, many schools don't have the supports in place to leverage existing technologies.

To address this need, Digi-Bridge works with community shareholders to assess need and identify opportunities. Our hardware and software agnostic approach means that you're getting recommendations that meet the needs of your school. Our work is mission-driven and we are not beholden to any market.
